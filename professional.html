Professional Experience

A brief overview of my professional experiences.

Manufacturing/Automation Engineering Intern - The Imagine Group

I started at The Imagine Group (Imagine) as a Summer 2024 intern with the engineering department. I was assigned a handful of projects that I could complete during the course of the summer. I will be working with Imagine when I have time off from school and returning as an intern with expanded projects in Summer 2025.

My projects focused on my robotics and automation knowledge to help improve consistency and costs. I had two major projects that took most of the eleven weeks of my first internship. I also completed three quicker projects.

Palletizer Insights

The facility's first palletizer wasn't being used much, so I was tasked with figuring out why and how we can alleviate any issues to increase its use. I found that it had a complicated setup process that required engineering time to complete, which meant the line it impacts is down for too long. There were also some small design issues that could be improved to suit Imagine's SKUs. Once I identified the problems and evaluated solutions, including costs, I presented the different options to the engineering team with my recommendation on a path forward.

Capital Concepts

The captical concepts project allowed me to get a taste for the administrative/financial side of engineering. I was tasked with identifying a few locations where future engineering projects could improve operations before determining rough costs to complete such a project. The aim was to spend a brief amount of time evaluating whether certain projects could have a reasonable ROI and would therefore be worth investing further time into determining a precise ROI.

Compactor Notifications

In my last few weeks, I completed a project aimed at reducing costs from emptying a trash compactor dumpster by ensuring it's full enough to be worth emptying. I used an AutomationDirect PLC to measure pressure in the hydraulic line to determine the dumpster fill level. If the fill level is above 80% specific Imagine employees will recieve an email notifying them of the fill state. A light also illuminates as a visual indicator for fill level and any errors.

Label Verification

When a large kitting run occurs, some kits were being misslabeled relative to their contents. A store in Minnesota might get the contents meant for a store in Massachusetts. My job was to develop a simple system that takes human eyes out of the equation when determining whether a pick-list matches a shipping label. I used an Omron camera with Machine Vision tools paired with a Raspberry Pi running a Node RED flow to act as a GUI. The structure I designed and built is intended to fit over the conveyors the kits roll along during the kitting process. This system is a low-cost solution aimed at preventing expenses from remaking kits that were sent to the wrong address.

Inspection Automation

This project is where I gained the most PLC programming experience. I programmed the machine provided by an integrator to scan cartons and identify defects. This machine is used for rejected batches to identify good cartons that could still be shipped. I worked with an AI powered camera to train what good parts should look like and it will identify the bad parts based on my training (highlighted areas in image below). The biggest learning experience involved lighting for the camera.
